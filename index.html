What I Would Suggest Strategically

1. Avoid Lowest-Cost Selection Logic

Hospitals are operational ecosystems. Poor coordination during design and MEP integration often creates exponentially higher downstream costs than consultant fees.

2. Select for Coordination Intelligence

The winning ecosystem should demonstrate evidence of interdisciplinary coordination, not just attractive drawings or isolated technical claims.

3. Prioritize Healthcare Documentation Maturity

Most failures in hospitals happen between architecture, HVAC, electrical, fire, biomedical, and operations—not in concept design.

4. Use Two-Stage Selection

Stage 1: Technical prequalification.
Stage 2: Scenario-based interview and coordination workshop.

5. Interview Through Problem Simulation

Instead of asking for portfolios alone, ask candidates to solve real project conflicts: - ICU airflow clash - Fire staircase integration - OT sterile movement sequencing - Basement smoke extraction

6. Keep Governance Transparent

Create a weighted evaluation matrix signed by the client to reduce future bias allegations and protect decision integrity.

Suggested Shortlist Structure

Rather than selecting a single consultant immediately, a more resilient strategy would be:

Stage 1: Technical shortlist (3–4 firms)
Stage 2: Live coordination workshop using real hospital coordination scenarios
Stage 3: Governance and accountability matrix evaluation
Stage 4: Commercial negotiation only after technical alignment

This sequence significantly reduces downstream redesign and coordination risks.
